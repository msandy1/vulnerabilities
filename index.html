What is LEGO?

LEGO is a line of plastic construction toys manufactured by The LEGO Group, a privately held company based in Billund, Denmark. The company's flagship product, LEGO, consists of interlocking plastic bricks accompanied by an array of gears, minifigures, and various other parts. These parts can be assembled and connected in many ways to construct objects, including vehicles, buildings, and working robots. Anything constructed can then be taken apart again, and the pieces reused for new constructions.

The word "LEGO" is derived from the Danish words "leg godt", meaning "play well". Later, it was discovered that "lego" also means "I put together" in Latin, though this was a mere coincidence.

A Brief History

The LEGO Group was founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Christiansen. It started out making wooden toys. The first plastic LEGO bricks were produced in 1949, and the "stud-and-tube" coupling system that makes the bricks interlock so effectively was patented in 1958.

Since then, LEGO has grown into a global phenomenon, inspiring generations of builders and creators with its endless possibilities. From simple bricks to complex robotic systems, LEGO continues to innovate and capture imaginations worldwide.
